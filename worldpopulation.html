World Population Analysis Using Python

Project Overview

This project uses Python libraries such as Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn to perform exploratory data analysis on global population trends. The aim was to understand how population growth has evolved across time, regions, and countries — and to present insights using meaningful visualizations. The dataset was sourced from Kaggle and contains population data for over 230 countries and territories spanning from 1970 to 2023. It includes Country and Continent Annual population data (1970–2023) Land area and population density Growth rate World population percentage

Objectives

• Identify the most and least populous countries in 2023
• Compare continent-wise population breakdown and global share
• Analyze historical population growth from 1970 to 2023
• Highlight top countries by growth rate using a heatmap

Tools Used

• Python: Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn
• Jupyter Notebook: For data exploration and visualization

Key Insights

Population Growth Over Time

This line chart shows the historical population growth of the top 5 most populous countries over the last five decades.
Countries like India and Nigeria show a steep upward curve, indicating fast population growth.
Russia and China have slower or flattening growth curves, suggesting demographic stabilization or decline.

World Population by Continent

This bar chart compares the average population of each continent across multiple decades.
Asia consistently remains the most populous continent.
Africa shows the most significant growth, indicating that it will play a larger global role in the future.
Europe and North America show relatively stable trends.

World Population Share by Continent

This pie chart illustrates how the world’s population is distributed across continents.
Asia holds over 50% of the world’s population.
Africa and Europe follow, while Oceania and South America have much smaller shares.

Top 20 Countries by Growth Rate

This heatmap highlights the 20 countries with the highest average annual population growth rate.
African countries dominate the top spots — such as Niger, Chad, and Uganda.
Countries with lower or even negative growth are largely absent from this chart, emphasizing regional differences.

Correlation Analysis of Population Features

Positive Correlation:
Yearly population columns (e.g., 1970, 1980, ..., 2023) show very high correlations with each other (close to 1.0), which is expected — population changes incrementally each year.
Area vs. Population:
Land area has a moderate positive correlation with total population — larger countries tend to have larger populations, but there are exceptions (e.g., large but sparsely populated countries like Canada).
Density vs. Area:
A negative correlation is often seen here — smaller countries can have higher population density (e.g., Bangladesh), while large countries may have lower density.
Growth Rate vs. Population:
The correlation between growth rate and population tends to be weak or slightly negative, suggesting that large populations don’t necessarily grow faster. Some smaller countries are actually growing more rapidly.

Conclusion

This analysis revealed clear demographic patterns across continents. It highlighted:
• Asia’s continued dominance in population share
• Africa’s rapid growth and future demographic importance
• Opportunities for deeper study into urbanization, density, and policy implications
The project demonstrates the power of Python for storytelling with data, and offers a solid base for more advanced demographic or geographic modeling in the future.
