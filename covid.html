COVID-19 Global Data Analysis

Project Overview

The COVID-19 pandemic reshaped every aspect of global society, and data has been key to understanding its scale and impact. In this project, I used MySQL to analyze global COVID-19 data and visualized the findings through a comprehensive Tableau dashboard. The project reveals global and regional trends in infections, deaths, and the spread of the virus.

Objectives

• Analyze global COVID-19 case and death statistics
• Identify countries with the highest infection and fatality rates
• Track infection trends over time
• Present insights using Tableau for interactive exploration

Tools & Technologies

• SQL (MySQL) for data querying and aggregation
• Tableau for data visualization and forecasting
• COVID-19 dataset with global daily case/death records

Key Insights

Global Summary of COVID-19 Cases and Deaths

Over 775 million confirmed cases and 7 million deaths globally, with a death rate of approximately 0.91%.

Countries with the Highest Total Deaths

The United States, Brazil, and India lead in total deaths, indicating varied healthcare responses.

Highest Infection Rate Relative to Population

Smaller nations had disproportionately high infection rates compared to their population size.

Tracking Infection Trends Over Time

Enabled forecasting trends by country using date-wise data, revealing peaks and future projections.

Tableau Dashboard Highlights

• Global KPIs: Total cases, deaths, and death percentage
• Bar Chart: Deaths per continent
• Map: Infection percentage by country
• Forecast Line Chart: Projected infection trends through 2026

Key Takeaways

• Europe and North America had the highest total deaths
• Small countries showed high infection percentages per capita
• Tableau forecasting showed stabilization in countries like the U.S. and spikes in others
